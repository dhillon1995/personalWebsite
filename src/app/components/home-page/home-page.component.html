E-foil Project

I’ve always had an interest in hydrofoils, so with the advent of electric foils coming to market I decided to build one. After gaining some experience in CAD and CFD software I started to experiment with some virtual models. This is an ongoing project, and it comes with a lot of challenges in computational simulation, composite manufacturing and basic electronics. Development Kit

At first I made this dev-kit so I could easily test a can-bus project I was working on at the time. I wanted to make a basic OBD2 digital dash using a Raspberry Pi-3 with a can-bus hat and a touchscreen. I wanted to keep all the cables external as I wanted to be able to change components quickly, with that in mind I iterated through 3 designs. This is a great example of prototype development driven by real world testing. Since then I have used this devkit to test various automation and IOT projects.

Carbon-fiber

This project’s objective is to test the viability of making Carbon Fiber at home utilizing a wet layup technique, and to test the different ways I can improve surface finish. The reason I decided to conduct this test is to test the whole process of making composite parts for more mechanically demanding projects. I used a simple negative draft mold of a small boat model. The mold was directly 3D printed out of PETG for strength & rigidity while remaining chemically unreactive throughout the process. There’s a lot to cover on this project so click more info to find out more.

Forged Carbon-Fiber

As above this one was more of a test of a manufacturing and design process rather than a complete project in itself. I wanted to test the process of both designing and creating a solid forged carbon piece. The mold making process is more complex for a forged carbon piece over a carbon fiber one due to the fact that the material needs to under compression to consolidate and cure in the correct structure. To address this, I designed a split-compression mold that compresses the material like a piston in a cylinder. Click view more information to read on.
